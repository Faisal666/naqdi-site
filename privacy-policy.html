Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

Naqdi ("we", "us", "the app") is an invoicing and expense-tracking app for Saudi freelancers. This policy explains what data we handle and what we don't.

1. What Naqdi stores on your device

Everything you create in Naqdi stays on your device by default. This includes:

  • Your business profile (name, phone, address, VAT number, IBAN)
  • Clients you add
  • Invoices you create and their line items
  • Expenses and receipt photos
  • Your signature (if you draw one for invoices)
  • Your business logo

This data is stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework. It isn't uploaded to any server we control.

2. What leaves your device

  • Apple: When you subscribe, Apple processes the payment. We don't see your card details. Apple shares subscription status with the app so we know you're a premium user.
  • Firebase (Google): Naqdi uses Firebase for:
    • Crashlytics: If the app crashes, an anonymous crash report is sent to Firebase so we can fix bugs. Includes device model, iOS version, and the stack trace. No business data.
    • Remote Config: The app fetches configuration values (minimum supported version, App Store URL) from Firebase. This is a one-way pull — nothing about you is sent.

3. What we never collect

  • Email addresses or passwords — Naqdi has no accounts.
  • Your location.
  • Your contact list.
  • Photos other than what you explicitly attach to an invoice or expense.

4. ZATCA e-invoices

Invoices you generate include a ZATCA-compliant QR code with seller information, VAT number, total, and timestamp as required by Saudi tax regulation. The QR code is generated on your device — it isn't reported to ZATCA automatically. Phase 2 (Integration) is not yet required for freelancers below SAR 7M revenue.

5. Your rights

  • You can view, edit, and delete all your data inside the app at any time.
  • Deleting the app removes all local data permanently.
  • For privacy questions or crash-report deletion requests, email us at: NaqdiAppFeedback@gmail.com

6. Changes

We'll update this policy if Naqdi starts handling data differently. The "Last updated" date above will change.
